1 Security Training
Let's start with how employees learn about staying safe online
When someone new joins your organization, do they get any training or guidance about security (like creating strong passwords or recognizing suspicious emails)?
After people have been working with you for a while, do they get reminders or updates about security practices?
Do you keep track of who has received security training?
2 Recognizing Suspicious Emails & Scams
This section is about "phishing" - fake emails trying to steal information or money
Do people in your organization know what phishing emails look like and how to spot them?
Examples: fake emails pretending to be from your bank, shipping notices you didn't order, urgent requests from "the boss"
If someone receives a suspicious email, do they know who to tell or what to do?
Have you ever tested your team with fake phishing emails to see if they can spot them?
Some organizations send safe test emails to help people practice
3 Written Rules & Guidelines
This is about having written policies for using computers, internet, and handling data
Does your organization have written rules or policies about computer and internet use?
Things like acceptable use policies, password requirements, how to handle customer data, etc.
Can employees easily find and read these policies when they need them?
Do you review and update these policies regularly?
4 Handling Security Problems
What happens if something goes wrong - like a virus, data breach, or ransomware
Do you have a plan for what to do if there's a security incident (like ransomware, a data breach, or someone's account gets hacked)?
Does everyone know who to contact immediately if something security-related goes wrong?
Like having a "break glass in case of emergency" contact list
Have you ever practiced or tested your response to a security incident?
Like a fire drill, but for cybersecurity
5 Passwords & Extra Security Steps
This covers passwords and "two-factor authentication" (getting a code on your phone when logging in)
Do you require "extra steps" to log into important systems (like a code sent to a phone or email)?
This is called "two-factor authentication" or "multi-factor authentication" (MFA)
Do you have rules about creating strong passwords?
Like requiring a certain length or using letters, numbers, and symbols
Do you encourage or require the use of password managers?
Apps like LastPass, 1Password, or Bitwarden that remember passwords securely
6 Personal Devices & Working Remotely
This is about using personal phones, laptops, or tablets for work
Do people use their personal phones, tablets, or computers for work?
If people use personal devices for work, do you require basic security measures?
Like requiring a PIN/password to unlock the phone, or keeping software updated
What happens to work data on personal devices when someone leaves the organization?
7 Leadership & Management Support
This looks at whether owners/managers take security seriously and set a good example
Do the owners/managers/leaders follow the same security practices they expect from employees?
Do they "practice what they preach"?
Do leaders talk about the importance of security and make it a priority?
Are resources (time, money, tools) allocated for security improvements?
8 Security Reminders & Updates
This is about keeping security top-of-mind through regular communication
How often do you send out security tips, reminders, or updates to employees?
Like emails, newsletters, or announcements about current threats or best practices
When you send security information, is it timely and relevant?
Like warning about current scams going around, seasonal threats (tax season phishing), etc.
Do you use multiple ways to communicate about security?
Like email, meetings, posters, chat messages, etc. - not just one method
